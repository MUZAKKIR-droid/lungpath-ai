Knowledge Base

Information regarding lung health, prevention, and detection standards.

Common Symptoms

Early-stage lung cancer often presents with subtle indicators. Recognized clinical symptoms include:

  • Persistent cough that intensifies over time.
  • Hemoptysis (coughing up blood or rust-colored sputum).
  • Localized chest pain exacerbated by deep respiration.
  • Dyspnea (shortness of breath) or unexplained fatigue.

Prevention & Risk Reduction

Statistically, smoking remains the primary etiological factor. Preventive measures include:

Tobacco Cessation

Immediate reduction in risk occurs upon cessation of smoking habits.

Environmental Safety

Testing for radon gas and avoiding occupational carcinogens like asbestos.

Clinical Importance

Early detection via low-dose CT screening significantly improves 5-year survival rates. LungPath AI serves as a preliminary analysis bridge for research and screening support.
